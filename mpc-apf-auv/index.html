Abstract Contributions Simulation Results

üèÜ Abstract

This paper presents the Nonlinear Model Predictive Control (NMPC) strategy for controlling the Omni Directional Intelligent Navigator (ODIN) embedded with an Artificial Potential Field (APF) algorithm for obstacle avoidance. NMPC is an optimal control method suitable for highly nonlinear systems like ODIN. The APF provides a simple yet effective approach for ODIN to operate safely in obstacle-dense marine environments. MATLAB simulations demonstrate effective trajectory tracking and obstacle avoidance with the proposed controller.

üß† Contributions

The main contribution of this research is to formulate an NMPC controller for ODIN underwater vehicle for trajectory tracking while utilizing APF approach for avoiding obstacles in the simulated marine environment.

üëâ Scenario: General 3D Navigation

The 3D trajectory visualization demonstrates successful obstacle avoidance capabilities of the proposed controller. The vehicle successfully navigates from an initial position at (0, 0, 0) to the target destination near (10, 12, 12) meters while avoiding three distinct obstacles positioned around the intended trajectory. The actual trajectory (blue line) closely follows the reference path (red line), indicating high tracking accuracy. The trajectory exhibits smooth curvature changes, particularly during the obstacle avoidance maneuvers, suggesting effective dynamic replanning capabilities. The evolution of barrier functions provides critical insight into the safety mechanisms employed by the system. All three obstacle barrier functions maintain a positive value during the whole simulation, indicates that collisions between the ODIN and the obstacles were avoided. This behavior confirms the controller role in maintaining safety constraints throughout the mission.

üëâ Trajectory Performance and Vertical Navigation

The simulation presents a mission profile focused on vertical climbing with obstacle avoidance. The vehicle executes a predominantly vertical ascent from an initial position near (0, 0, 0) meters to a destination at approximately (0, 0, 12) meters, representing a 12-meter vertical climb. Two obstacles are strategically positioned at around z=3m and z=8m, directly obstructing the vertical reference path. The ODIN trajectory exhibits characteristic "S-curve" patterns around each obstacle, indicating smooth avoidance maneuvers. Nevertheless, the avoidance paths taken in the simulation were not optimized in terms of the total distance traveled.
